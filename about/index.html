We are a public health accelerator with one mission: save lives

Resolve to Save Lives helps governments and civil society implement scalable, proven strategies.

We are determined to prevent millions of deaths from cardiovascular disease and epidemics, with a focus on accelerating action through speed, simplicity, and scale in low- and middle-income countries.

Resolve to Save Lives is an initiative of the global health non-profit organization Vital Strategies and we received $225 million from Bloomberg Philanthropies, the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

President Dr. Tom Frieden, former director of the US Centers for Disease Control, explains the mission of Resolve to Save Lives

We are funders and operators

Download a 1-pager about Resolve to Save Lives

We provide catalytic funding to countries interested in improving epidemic preparedness or their citizens’ heart health. And, we also staff the world’s leading experts in cardiovascular health and epidemic preparedness, so that we can send them into the field to provide on-the-ground technical assistance.

We operate in dozens of countries

We work primarily through our partners’ country offices. Our implementing partners include the World Health Organization, World Bank, CDC Foundation, Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health, the Campaign for Tobacco Free Kids, and Vital Strategies.

Our focus is on accelerating action through speed, simplicity, and scale in low- and middle-income countries

What we can and can't do

We can do:

  • Provide guidance and technical support in program design and implementation — what to do and how to do it
  • Assistance in securing program funding
  • Assistance in identifying and engaging partners, in-country and globally
  • Catalyze progress

We can't do:

  • Run country programs
  • Pay for programs
  • Set country health and NCD prevention policy
  • Sustain programs long-term

Team

Our team of global health experts works with governments to help them implement evidence-based strategies that allow their citizens to lead the healthiest lives possible.

The New York City-based initiative is led by Dr. Tom Frieden, former director of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, and is housed within Vital Strategies.
